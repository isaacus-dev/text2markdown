Part II — Definitions and Interpretation
For the purposes of this Instrument:
“Agreement” means this Instrument as binding between the Parties.
“Confidential Information” means any information marked “confidential” or that a reasonable person would treat as confidential.
“Effective Date” means 01 February 2024.
“Services” means the managed data processing and hosting services described in Schedule 1.
“Force Majeure Event” means an event beyond a party’s reasonable control (including acts of God, war, strikes, or Internet backbone failure).
law means any statute, regulation, or decision of a competent authority (including, without limitation, the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR)).
References to Sections, Subsections, Paragraphs, Clauses, Items, Schedules, Annexes, Appendices, Exhibits, and Figures are to those of this Instrument unless otherwise stated.
Where a range is cited (e.g., “ss 2.1(a)–2.1(c)”), it includes the start and end items, inclusive.
In this document, “<” and “>” and “&” denote their ordinary textual meanings (i.e., 5 < 7 & 7 > 5), not markup.

Part III — Operative Provisions

Section 1 — Grant of License
Clause 1.1. Licensor hereby grants to Licensee a non-exclusive, revocable, non-transferable license to use the Services solely in Melbourne, Victoria, Australia, the European Union, and the United States.
1.2. The scope of the license is subject to the “Confidential Information” restrictions and the Service Levels in Schedule 1.
1.3 The Licensee acknowledges that if they fail to comply with the requirement that they 'shall pay all invoices within 30 days of invoice date', the Licensor may terminate this Instrument immediately upon written notice to the Licensee.
1.4.. The Licensee will comply with the Act.

Section 2 — Data Protection
Introductory paragraph (decision-style, unnumbered): The tribunal notes that data processing must be lawful, fair, and transparent.

2.1 Subsection — Lawful Basis.
(a) Item. Processing shall rely on at least one lawful basis as per GDPR Article 6(1)(b).
(b) Item. For U.S. users, processing must comply with Title 18 of the United States Code, including Section 1030(a)(5)(B).
(c) Item. Cross-reference: see Section 3 and ss 2.1(a)–2.1(c).

2.2 Subsection — Cross-References.
Paragraph. See Part IV, Clause 4.2, and Schedule 1 Table A-1; also refer to Annex A §A.3 and Exhibit X, and Figure 1.

Section 3 — Payment, Delivery & Expiry
3.1 Paragraph — Payment. The Licensee shall pay all invoices within 30 days of invoice date. The next payment is due on 15 March 2024.
3.2 Paragraph — Delivery. Initial delivery of Services will occur on 20 March 2024 to the data center at 123 Example Street, Docklands VIC 3008, Australia.
3.3 Paragraph — Expiry. This Instrument expires at close of business on 01 February 2026, unless earlier terminated under Clause 4.1.
