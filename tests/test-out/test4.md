## <a id="seg-28"></a>Part II — Definitions and Interpretation

For the purposes of this Instrument:<br>
“Agreement” means this Instrument as binding between the Parties.<br>
“Confidential Information” means any information marked “confidential” or that a reasonable person would treat as confidential.<br>
“Effective Date” means 01 February 2024.<br>
“Services” means the managed data processing and hosting services described in [Schedule 1](#seg-67).<br>
“Force Majeure Event” means an event beyond a party’s reasonable control (including acts of God, war, strikes, or Internet backbone failure).<br>
law means any statute, regulation, or decision of a competent authority (including, without limitation, the *Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)*  and *Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR)).*<br>
References to Sections, Subsections, Paragraphs, Clauses, Items, Schedules, Annexes, Appendices, Exhibits, and Figures are to those of this Instrument unless otherwise stated.<br>
Where a range is cited (e.g., “ss 2.1(a)–2.1(c)”), it includes the start and end items, inclusive.<br>
In this document, “<” and “>” and “&” denote their ordinary textual meanings (i.e., 5 < 7 & 7 > 5), not markup.<br>

## <a id="seg-41"></a>Part III — Operative Provisions

### <a id="seg-42"></a>Section 1 — Grant of License

Clause 1.1. Licensor hereby grants to Licensee a non-exclusive, revocable, non-transferable license to use the Services solely in Melbourne, Victoria, Australia, the European Union, and the United States.<br>
1.2. The scope of the license is subject to the “Confidential Information” restrictions and the Service Levels in [Schedule 1](#seg-67).<br>
1.3 The Licensee acknowledges that if they fail to comply with the requirement that they 'shall pay all invoices within 30 days of invoice date', the Licensor may terminate this Instrument immediately upon written notice to the Licensee.<br>
1.4.. The Licensee will comply with the *Act* .<br>

Introductory paragraph (decision-style, unnumbered): The tribunal notes that data processing must be lawful, fair, and transparent.<br>
